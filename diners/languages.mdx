Rumizi menus can be translated into several languages. Whether a specific
venue supports your language depends on what the owner set up.

## Pick your language

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the menu header">
    Tap the language button in the header — usually a globe or a small
    flag.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick from the list">
    You'll see every language the venue supports. Tap to switch.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The menu updates">
    Item names, descriptions, and category labels re-render in your
    chosen language immediately. Tags like "Vegetarian" switch too.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Your choice is remembered on your phone for that venue. Next time you
scan the same QR code, it opens in the language you picked.
</Tip>

## Supported languages

Today Rumizi supports:

- English
- Spanish
- Italian
- Arabic (right-to-left)
- Hindi
- Persian / Farsi (right-to-left)
- French
- Chinese
- Punjabi
- Tagalog

A venue picks which ones to enable. Not every venue offers all ten.

## What if my language isn't in the list?

The venue doesn't have translations for your language yet. You'll see
the menu in the venue's **default language** — usually whatever the
country of the venue speaks. You can still use every feature, including
the [menu assistant](/diners/menu-assistant), to understand the menu.

## What if some items aren't translated?

Missing translations fall back to the venue's default language — so
you might see an item name in English inside an otherwise-Spanish menu.
Nothing is ever blank.
