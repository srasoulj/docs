Quick answers for diners. If something isn't covered, ask your server —
they have direct support from Rumizi.

## The basics

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Do I need to install an app?">
    No. Rumizi opens in your phone's browser when you scan the QR code.
    There's nothing to install.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do I need an account?">
    No. Scanning, browsing, adding to cart, and ordering together all
    work without signing in. Some venues offer an optional account for
    community features — see [Accounts](/diners/accounts).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is there a cost?">
    No. Rumizi is free for diners — you just scan the QR and use the
    menu.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Does it work offline?">
    You need an internet connection to load the menu the first time.
    After that, most things keep working on a spotty connection because
    your cart is stored on your phone.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Using the menu

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I change the language?">
    Tap the language button in the header. You'll see every language
    the venue supports. Details: [Switching language](/diners/languages).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I switch to dark mode?">
    Tap the theme toggle in the header (sun / moon icon). Your choice
    is remembered on your phone for that venue.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="An item looks dimmed and I can't add it.">
    It's marked out of stock by the venue. The item is still shown so
    you know what it normally is, but you can't add it to your cart
    right now.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Does the cart actually send my order to the kitchen?">
    It depends on the venue. Some Rumizi venues use the cart as a live
    order; others use it as a list you show your server. Ask your
    server if you're not sure.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Order together

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I start a group cart?">
    Tap **Order together** in the menu header, then share the link or
    QR code that appears. Details: [Order together](/diners/order-together).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I leave a group cart?">
    Yes. Tap **Leave group** in the header or in the cart dialog. You're
    removed; everyone else's items stay untouched.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How long does a group last?">
    About seven days, then the link expires and the cart is cleaned up.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## The menu assistant

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Is the assistant answering from the actual menu?">
    Yes — it reads the same menu you see and only suggests dishes
    that are really on it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I trust it for allergen questions?">
    For anything that affects your health, confirm with your server.
    The assistant reads the venue's own allergen info, which is usually
    right, but a human server is the safest final check.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Will it add things to my cart automatically?">
    No. Every cart change needs a final **Apply** tap from you — the
    assistant drafts the change, you confirm it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I clear the chat?">
    Tap **Clear chat** in the assistant panel. Your cart is unaffected.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Privacy

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Does Rumizi track me?">
    Rumizi stores what you need to use the menu — your cart, your
    language choice, your theme, and (if you sign in) your account at a
    venue. It doesn't sell or share that data.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Who sees what I add to a group cart?">
    Only other members of that same group cart. Not the venue next door,
    not Rumizi, not anyone else.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
