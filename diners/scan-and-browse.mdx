You don't install anything to use a Rumizi menu. If the restaurant has a
QR code on the table, your phone already has everything it needs.

## Open the menu

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your camera">
    Use the built-in camera app on iPhone or Android. No special QR
    scanner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Point it at the QR code">
    Hold the camera over the code for a second. A link appears at the
    top (on iPhone) or as a notification (on Android).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap the link">
    The menu opens in your browser. That's the whole setup.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Can't scan? Ask your server for the venue's Rumizi link. It's just
`rumizi.com/venue-name`.
</Tip>

## Find what you want

The menu is organised in categories — *Starters*, *Mains*, *Drinks*, and
so on. A few things you can do to find dishes faster:

- **Scroll the Popular row at the top.** Venues pin their best dishes
  here.
- **Tap a tag filter** (Vegetarian, Spicy, Gluten-free) to narrow the
  menu down.
- **Search.** The search box matches item names and ingredients.
- **Switch language** from the header if the default isn't yours. See
  [Languages](/diners/languages).

## Item detail

Tap any dish to open its detail page. You'll see:

- A photo (if the venue added one).
- The full description and price.
- Any allergen or dietary tags.
- **Modifiers** — pick a size, spice level, or add-ons.
- **Goes well with** — the venue's suggestions for what pairs with it.
- **Add to cart** — the only action you need to keep going.

## Your cart

Adding an item opens a cart indicator at the bottom of the screen. Tap it
to see everything you've added, change quantities, or remove items.

<Note>
On most Rumizi venues, the cart is a **draft** — a list of what you
intend to order. Whether the dish actually goes to the kitchen depends on
the venue. Some places use Rumizi's cart to take orders at the table
(your server confirms with you first); others use it as a "build your
order, then tell your server" tool. Ask your server if you're not sure.
</Note>

## Out of stock items

If a dish is dimmed and marked "Unavailable", the kitchen is out.
Tapping it still shows the detail page so you can see what it is, but
you can't add it to your cart.

## Finish up

When you're happy with your cart, hand your phone to your server or read
the items out. The venue decides how the final hand-off works — Rumizi's
cart is there to make it easy for you to remember what you picked.
