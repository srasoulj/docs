Dinner with a group? You don't have to shout your order across the table.
Rumizi has a shared-cart mode that lets everyone add their own items to
the same order from their own phone.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="One person starts a group">
    Whoever scanned the menu first taps **Order together** in the
    header. That creates a shared cart on the server and opens a dialog
    with a link and a QR code.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Everyone else joins">
    Share the link in your group chat, or let people at the table scan
    the QR code with their camera. Each join attaches that person's
    browser to the same cart.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Everyone adds what they want">
    Each person picks their own dishes from their own phone. Items
    appear in the shared cart under that person's name.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The host reviews the final cart">
    The person who started the group can see everything added by
    everyone. They hand the phone to the server when it's time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
The share link is a normal web URL. Send it over WhatsApp, iMessage,
Telegram, or just read it out loud — whatever your group uses.
</Tip>

## How long a group lasts

A group cart is valid for about **seven days**, which covers a long
dinner, a late night, and even a second visit the next day. After that
the link expires and the cart is cleaned up.

## If you want to leave

Changed your mind and want to order on your own? Open the menu and tap
**Leave group** in the header (or in the cart dialog). You're removed
from the shared cart, and your next addition starts a fresh personal
cart on your phone.

<Note>
Leaving the group only affects **your own** phone. The group and
everyone else's items are untouched. Nobody else sees that you left.
</Note>

## Things to know

- You don't need an account. Group carts work for anonymous diners.
- Everyone can see everyone else's items as they get added — it's live.
- Your own items are stored on your phone and synced to the shared cart
  automatically, so you keep your list even if the connection drops for
  a second.
- If you add a modifier or a note to an item, other members see it too.
